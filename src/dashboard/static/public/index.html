About This Station

What is this?

The Mosswood Science Station is a private citizen science installation at a residential property in Berkeley, California. All data shown here is collected by our own sensors and is made freely available for educational and research purposes.

Seismograph

We operate a Raspberry Shake RS4D — a professional-grade seismograph. The instrument streams continuous data at 100 Hz. Our EQ Prediction Engine applies STA/LTA (Short-Term Average / Long-Term Average) analysis to detect and classify seismic events in real time.

BirdNET

Acoustic monitoring uses BirdNET-Analyzer (Cornell Lab of Ornithology) running locally on a Raspberry Pi 5. Multiple microphones cover the property perimeter and garden. Species detections are published in near real-time. Location metadata is intentionally withheld from this public feed.

Air Quality

AirGradient ONE monitors measure CO₂ (SCD40), PM2.5 (PMS5003), temperature, humidity, TVOC, and NOx. Data is published directly over WiFi to our local MQTT broker.

Data Policy

All data shown here is from sensors on private residential property. Personal data (occupancy, internal cameras, personal schedules) is never published. This public feed contains only environmental and scientific measurements.
